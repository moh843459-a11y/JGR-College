Institutional Profile

Established uniquely in 2014 within Blackburn, United Kingdom, by the visionary scholar Mufti Muhammad Nizamuddin Misbahi, JGR College (جامعة غوثية رضوية) functions as an elite center for traditional higher Islamic learning.

The operational framework of our curriculum rigorously executes the historically verified Dars e Nizami matrix—the foundational intellectual golden standard endorsed and implemented across prominent seminaries of the Indian Subcontinent.

Through comprehensive systemic guidance, our alumni have evolved into prominent young thinkers, leading structural branches of various madāris, and directly safeguarding communities while serving dynamically as Imams and Academic Mentors.

Societal Impact

We combine deep classical text comprehension with essential leadership methodologies to ensure our graduated delegation serves as highly articulate, resilient assets for global civic settings.

The Directorate

Mufti Muhammad Nizamuddin Misbahi

Founder & Principal Headteacher

Our Principal is an elite graduate and distinguished alumnus of the internationally acclaimed Islamic University, Al Jāmiah Tul Ashrafiyyah located in Mubarakpur, UP, India. Possessing an illustrious instructional tenure traversing nearly 25 operational years, he commands immense authority as a global specialist across Fiqh (Jurisprudence), Tafsir (Exegesis), Hadith (Prophetic Traditions), and secondary rational sciences.

As a visionary textual archivist, Mufti Muhammad Nizamuddin Misbahi has contributed deeply to global academic heritage by editing, annotating, and publishing critical historical treatises (*Tahqiq*) that were originally penned hundreds if not a thousand years ago. This ensures classical scholarship is accurately preserved for contemporary study.

A multi-lingual orator, he delivers complex jurisprudential directives and public lectures fluently across multiple linguistic spheres, including Urdu, Arabic, and English, enabling successful interaction across vast generational socio-cultural demographics.
